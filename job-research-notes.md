# Job Research Notes – Data Analyst Roles

**Activity:** Researching entry-level data analyst job adverts  
**Date:** April 2026  
**Sources:** LinkedIn Jobs, Indeed.co.uk  

---

## Why I did this

As part of preparing for my career after graduation, I looked at real job adverts for entry-level and graduate data analyst roles. The goal was to understand what employers actually want, so I can compare that to my own current skills and know what to focus on.

---

## Jobs I looked at

I searched for "graduate data analyst" and "junior data analyst" on LinkedIn and Indeed. I looked at 5 adverts in total. Here is a summary of what came up most often.

---

## Most commonly required skills

| Skill | How often mentioned |
|---|---|
| Microsoft Excel | All 5 adverts |
| Communication skills (written and verbal) | All 5 adverts |
| Attention to detail | 4 out of 5 |
| SQL (database querying) | 4 out of 5 |
| Python or R (basic level) | 3 out of 5 |
| Data visualisation (e.g. Power BI, Tableau) | 3 out of 5 |
| Ability to work in a team | 3 out of 5 |
| Problem solving | 4 out of 5 |

---

## What employers said about degrees

Most adverts said a degree in a relevant subject (Computer Science, Data Science, Maths, Statistics or similar) was required or preferred. Some said they would consider any degree if the candidate had strong analytical skills and relevant experience or projects.

---

## My honest skills comparison

| Skill | My current level | What I need to do |
|---|---|---|
| Excel | Basic – can use it for simple tasks | Practise more, learn VLOOKUP and pivot tables |
| Communication | Growing – practised in group project and mock interview | Keep practising, especially giving specific examples |
| SQL | Very basic – covered briefly in lectures | Do a free online SQL course (e.g. W3Schools, Khan Academy) |
| Python | Basic – used in AI coursework | Continue building on module learning |
| Data visualisation | Limited | Try a free Power BI or Tableau tutorial |
| Teamwork | Developing – evidenced in group project | Already doing this |

---

## What I took away from this research

The biggest gap I need to address is SQL and data visualisation. These came up in most adverts but are areas I have not practised much outside of lectures. I also need to be able to talk confidently in interviews about examples of using data, which my mock interview practice is helping with.

Having my AI coursework and group project on my CV will help, even if the projects are at a beginner level, because they show I have done real work with data and AI tools.
