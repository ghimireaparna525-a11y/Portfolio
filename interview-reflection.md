# Mock Interview Reflection – Data Analyst Role

**Activity:** Practice job interview conducted by my tutor  
**Role practiced:** Data Analyst  
**Date:** April 2026  

---

## What was this activity?

My tutor conducted a mock interview with me for a data analyst job role. This was a practice session designed to help me experience what a real job interview feels like and to get feedback on how I perform.

## How did I prepare?

Before the interview I spent some time reading about what data analysts do day to day. I looked at a few job adverts to understand what skills employers typically ask for, such as Excel, data interpretation, problem solving and communication. I also thought about experiences from my studies that I could talk about, such as working in a group and completing coursework on AI.

## What happened in the interview?

My tutor asked me questions about my skills, my experience with data and AI, why I was interested in data analysis, and how I handle challenges. I tried to answer using examples from my university work where I could.

## What feedback did I receive?

My tutor gave me verbal feedback after the session. The main points were:

- I need to give more specific examples when answering questions, rather than speaking generally. For example, instead of saying "I am good at working in teams," I should say "In my group project I was responsible for researching chatbot systems and delivering the findings to the team by a set deadline."
- My answers were clear and easy to understand, which is a positive.
- I should practise using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to structure my answers better.

## What will I do differently?

I will practise answering common interview questions using the STAR method before any future interviews. I will also make a short list of specific examples from my studies that I can use as evidence in interviews.

## Why does this matter for my career?

Interviews are how you get jobs. Practising in a safe environment with honest feedback is one of the best things I can do at this stage. Even though it felt uncomfortable, I am glad I did it because I now know what I need to work on before I start applying for real positions after graduation.
