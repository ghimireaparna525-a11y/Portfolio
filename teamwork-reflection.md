# Teamwork Reflection – Group Project

**Activity:** Working as a team member on the Conversational AI Agent group project  
**Role:** Team member (supporting the team leader)  
**Duration:** February 2026 – ongoing  

---

## What was this activity?

As part of my final year, I have been working in a group to build a conversational AI agent for citizen support. I am not the team leader — my role has been to support the team leader by completing tasks that are assigned to me, attending meetings, and contributing ideas.

## What tasks have I been responsible for?

- Researching existing citizen-facing AI systems (such as GOV.UK and NHS 111 online) and writing up summaries for the team
- Creating a dataset of example citizen questions and answers in CSV format, which the team used in the project
- Designing the exhibition poster in Canva to present our project publicly
- Attending group meetings and contributing to discussions about the project direction

## What have I learnt about teamwork?

Working in a team where someone else is the leader has taught me a lot. Some of the key things I have learnt:

**Communication matters.** Early on I did not always ask for help when I was unsure about a task. I learnt that it is better to ask a question early than to do the wrong thing and waste time. I started communicating more openly with the team leader about progress.

**Deadlines affect the whole group.** When one person is late with their task, it holds everyone else up. Being part of a team made me more aware of my own deadlines because I could see directly how my work fed into someone else's work.

**You do not have to lead to contribute.** At first I thought being a team member rather than the leader was less important. I realised that reliable, well-done contributions from every team member are what make a project succeed.

## What would I do differently?

I would get more involved in the technical side of the project earlier rather than mainly focusing on research and design tasks. I would also ask to join code review sessions or testing sessions even if coding is not my main role, just to understand the technical side better.

## Relevance to my career

Almost every job involves working in a team. Being able to follow instructions, meet deadlines, communicate honestly and contribute reliably are skills that employers value just as much as technical skills. This project has helped me develop and reflect on all of these.
