# AI Security Report – Reflection

**Module:** Artificial Intelligence  
**University:** University of Bradford  
**Date Submitted:** March 2026  

---

## What was this activity?

I wrote a report on AI security as part of my module assessment. The report looked at the risks and threats that come with building and using AI systems.

## What did I research and write about?

I researched several key AI security topics including:

- **Adversarial attacks** – where someone deliberately tricks an AI model by feeding it slightly altered inputs. For example, changing a few pixels in an image so that an AI misidentifies it completely.
- **Data poisoning** – where bad data is put into a training dataset to make the AI model behave incorrectly.
- **Bias in AI** – where an AI system produces unfair or discriminatory results because of biased training data.
- **Privacy risks** – where personal data used to train AI models could be leaked or misused.

## What did I learn?

Before writing this report I had not thought much about the risks of AI. I assumed AI was mostly a positive tool. Writing this report made me realise that AI systems can be manipulated or misused, and that security and ethics need to be thought about from the very beginning of any AI project, not just added on at the end.

I also learnt how to structure a formal report with an introduction, main findings and a conclusion, and how to reference academic sources properly.

## How does this connect to my group project?

Our group is building a conversational AI agent for citizen support. This report made me think about how important it is that our agent gives accurate information and cannot be easily manipulated by users trying to get incorrect outputs. Security is relevant even in a student project.

## Relevance to my career

AI security and responsible AI is a growing area that employers care about. Understanding the risks of AI systems makes me a more thoughtful and aware developer or analyst, even at an early stage of my career.
